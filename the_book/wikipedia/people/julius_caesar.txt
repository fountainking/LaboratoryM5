Julius Caesar

Gaius Julius Caesar was a Roman general
and statesman. A member of the First
Triumvirate, Caesar led the Roman
armies in the Gallic Wars before
defeating his political rival Pompey in
a civil war. He subsequently became
dictator from 49 BC until his
assassination in 44 BC. Caesar played a
critical role in the events that led to
the demise of the Roman Republic and
the rise of the Roman Empire.
