Tuna

A tuna is a saltwater fish that belongs
to the tribe Thunnini, a subgrouping of
the Scombridae (mackerel) family. The
Thunnini comprise 15 species across
five genera, the sizes of which vary
greatly, ranging from the bullet tuna
up to the Atlantic bluefin tuna, which
averages 2 m (6.6 ft) and is believed
to live up to 50 years.
