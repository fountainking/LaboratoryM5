Dolphin

A dolphin is a common name used for
some of the aquatic mammals in the
cetacean clade Odontoceti, the toothed
whales. Dolphins belong to the families
Delphinidae, along with the river
dolphin families Platanistidae,
Iniidae, Pontoporiidae, and probably
extinct Lipotidae. There are 40 extant
species named as dolphins.
