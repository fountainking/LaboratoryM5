Plato

Plato was an ancient Greek philosopher
of the Classical period who is
considered a foundational thinker in
Western philosophy and an innovator of
the written dialogue and dialectic
forms. He influenced all the major
areas of theoretical philosophy and
practical philosophy, and was the
founder of the Platonic Academy, a
philosophical school in Athens where
Plato taught the doctrines that would
later become known as Platonism.
