Kazakhstan

Kazakhstan, officially the Republic of
Kazakhstan, is a landlocked country
primarily in Central Asia, with a
portion in Eastern Europe. It borders
Russia to the north and west, China to
the east, Kyrgyzstan to the southeast,
Uzbekistan to the south, and
Turkmenistan to the southwest, with a
coastline along the Caspian Sea. Its
capital is Astana, while the largest
city and leading cultural and
commercial hub is Almaty. It used to be
the Capital city from 1929 to 1997,
then later the Capital was moved from
Almaty to Akmola, which is now known as
Astana.
