Saint Kitts and Nevis

Saint Kitts and Nevis, officially the
Federation of Saint Kitts and Nevis, is
an island country located in the
Caribbean consisting of the two islands
of Saint Kitts and Nevis, in the
Leeward Islands chain of the Lesser
Antilles. With 261 square kilometres
(101 sq mi) of territory, and roughly
48,000 inhabitants, it is the smallest
sovereign state in the Western
Hemisphere, in both area and
population, as well as the world's
smallest sovereign federation. The
country is a Commonwealth realm, with
Charles III as king and head of state.
