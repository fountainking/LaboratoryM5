Amazon rainforest

The Amazon rainforest, also called the
Amazon jungle or Amazonia, is a moist
broadleaf tropical rainforest in the
Amazon biome that covers most of the
Amazon basin of South America. This
basin encompasses 7 million km2 (2.7
million sq mi), of which 6 million km2
(2.3 million sq mi) are covered by the
rainforest. This region includes
territory belonging to nine nations and
3,344 indigenous territories.
