Carl Jung

Carl Gustav Jung was a Swiss
psychiatrist, psychotherapist, and
psychologist who founded the school of
analytical psychology. A prolific
author of over twenty books,
illustrator, and correspondent, Jung
was a complex and convoluted academic,
best known for his concept of
archetypes. Alongside contemporaries
Sigmund Freud and Alfred Adler, Jung
became one of the most influential
psychologists of the early 20th century
and has fostered not only scholarship,
but also popular interest.
