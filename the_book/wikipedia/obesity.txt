Obesity

Obesity is a medical condition,
considered by multiple organizations to
be a disease, in which excess body fat
has accumulated to such an extent that
it can have negative effects on health.
People are classified as obese when
their body mass index (BMI)a person's
weight divided by the square of the
person's heightis over 30kg/m2; the
range 2530kg/m2 is defined as
overweight. Some East Asian countries
use lower values to calculate obesity.
Obesity is a major cause of disability
and is correlated with various diseases
and conditions, particularly
cardiovascular diseases, type 2
diabetes, obstructive sleep apnea,
certain types of cancer, and
osteoarthritis.
